Very big achievements. DUNA!

 MY Duna experience. I went to duna with a 10,000 Δv, Way too much ( Matt Lowne can use that to do a trip to all Jool’s moons. ) but the hardest part was getting an encounter. At that point I was an ameture at manover nodes, and I fiddled around, eating up 5000 Delta -V. FInally, I got on an encounter, then I wasted 2000 DV slowing down, and used minimal aerobraking. The kerbals are still stuck there to this day.

DOCKING. I used the Recently , and Jovially Christened “The Matt Lowne Method, ot the MTM Not to be confused with the moho/mercury transfer module” ( you can guess who I look up to for KSP now ). Basically, you use the “ Target” node on SAS, and then point both ships towards each other. They will stay pointed towards each other, and slowly drift towards each other. Another tip ( from Me ) is make sure that your prograde marker is overlapping your target marker, so that means that you are going towards your target. Finally, The ports actually have built in magnets, so if you leave them they will auto - attract. 

JOOL. Jool is a very recent one for me, and the tip is to use the new MTM ( Moho transfer module ) for your base ship, if not, use xenon thrusters anyway. Why? You ask. The MTM has built in xenon tanks, make sure to use RTGs because there is not enough sun, and pack a lot of batteries. ( also, the MTM looks cool. )
Jool orbit is ludicrously fast, at nearly 1000m/s. The MTM can also survive going into the Julian atmosphere, but your relay dishes and science and Power sources will not. Also, the MTM can house xenon thrusters, four of them, actually, and packs a punch. Be sure to either start burning really early for your jool capture, or use tylo, or have a Lf/Ox Engine, or you will zip past jool ( Unless if your periapsis takes a year to reach ( 80% of the time it does )), but have a Liquid engine Nonetheless. 

LATHE. My Most recent adventure, or second recent, depending if you count a puny rover. Lathe should be attempted with 7000 DV, unless if you are matt lowne, then 4000 will do ( at takeoff ) N.E.R.V engines are preferred because liquid fuel is lighter that LF/OX, and that the N.E.R.V engine is very efficient. Xenon engines also will do, but they have abysmal thrust, at only 2 KN, compared to the 60 KN offered by the NERV. The NERV is still puny, as the biggest engine in non-modded KSP is at 4000 KN, and is somewhat of a replica of the NASA SLS core stage, and the most strong SRB sits at an astounding 3336 KN and is a replica of the space shuttle SRB. But, back to Lathe. You need a good heat shield, and some mining equipment, or you will be a burnt crisp on the surface of Lathe, or even worse, Your Immortal Kerbals ( did I mention they didn't need air, water or food? ) will be stranded on a kerbin-like planet forever. Lathe is basically a kerbin, but in orbit of Jool. Some sights to see are Tylo, which frequently is visible, and Vall, and of course, Jool itself.


Space Stations. Space stations and docking is like the Next level in KSP. If you can construct a station, that marks you into the Pro category Of KSP. for tips, see docking. How I make stations is that I make most of the station in a single launch, and then send up the parts that I forgot, Like some RTGs, or more fuel. Yeah.. 

Rovers. One of the hardest things to make for a noob KSPer, and that includes me. You might think of a fancy skycrane, ect… but if reality in KSP, all you need to do is assemble one of your usual landers, and strap a rover on it. Eezy. Well, Mabey not that easy, but wheels have a surprisingly high impact tolerance, and rovers have wheels.
Modeled rockets. Many rockets are modeled after historic engines. For example, the Clydesdale booster is modeled after the space shuttle booster, The Poodle LF/OX engine iftar ESA’s Certuar, either single or double bell, The Mainsail engine after ESA’s Ariane 5 main core, the Kickback after ESA’s Ariane 5 booster, Mammoth after NASA’s SLS Core stage, and the Panther afterburning jet engine after the F-15 ( correct me if i’m wrong. ) KSP’s Last update was a partnership with ESA, and NASA has also been KSP’s partners, with the Asteroid Redirect As one of the ingame MIssions, and also a rough copy of APOLLO in the game, and also a nearly exact comp of the Lunar descent module. There is also the Mercury Observation module, and the Mercury transfer modules, among other things.

Something About Rockets

I am a big fan of SpaceX and NASA!

FALCON 9
Falcon 9 is a two-stage rocket designed and manufactured by SpaceX for the reliable and safe transport of satellites and the Dragon spacecraft into orbit. Falcon 9 is the first orbital class rocket capable of reflight. SpaceX believes rocket reusability is the key breakthrough needed to reduce the cost of access to space and enable people to live on other planets.

Falcon 9 was designed from the ground up for maximum reliability. Falcon 9’s simple two-stage configuration minimizes the number of separation events -- and with nine first-stage engines, it can safely complete its mission even in the event of an engine shutdown.

Falcon 9 made history in 2012 when it delivered Dragon into the correct orbit for rendezvous with the International Space Station, making SpaceX the first commercial company ever to visit the station. Since then Falcon 9 has made numerous trips to space, delivering satellites to orbit as well as delivering and returning cargo from the space station for NASA. Falcon 9, along with the Dragon spacecraft, was designed from the outset to deliver humans into space and under an agreement with NASA, SpaceX is actively working toward this goal.

Falcon Heavy Launched a Telsla Roadster and its driver Starman, into the orbit around the sun in Feb,2018 

When Falcon Heavy lifted off, it became the most powerful operational rocket in the world by a factor of two. With the ability to lift into orbit nearly 64 metric tons (141,000 lb)---a mass greater than a 737 jetliner loaded with passengers, crew, luggage and fuel--Falcon Heavy can lift more than twice the payload of the next closest operational vehicle, the Delta IV Heavy, at one-third the cost.

Following liftoff, the two side boosters separated from the center core and returned to landing site for future reuse.

Falcon Heavy put a Tesla Roadster and its passenger, Starman, into orbit around the sun. At max velocity Starman and the Roadster will travel 11 km/s (7mi/s) and travel 400 million km (250 million mi) from Earth.
